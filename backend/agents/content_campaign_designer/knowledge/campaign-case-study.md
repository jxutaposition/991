# Reference Case: Yarn Voice Campaign

Ran 3 campaigns for Yarn's auto-audio-generation product with ~200 active creators.

## Mechanic
Built demo assets using prebuilt audio profiles → sent creators a landing page to record a few seconds of their voice → generated a personalized demo in their voice → tracked who completed and posted.

## Tracking Funnel
sent → opened → confirmed → submitted → published

## Results
10-20 posts out of 200 active creators per campaign. Not bad for a cold mechanic, not good enough to continue.

## Why It Stopped
- Most creators disliked posting the same content as everyone else — homogeneity killed motivation
- Some had concerns about the likeness of their cloned voice
- 3 campaigns, same declining pattern each time

## Lessons
1. Personalization at content level doesn't substitute for message-level personalization
2. Homogeneous campaigns have a ceiling — first few creators get engagement, rest is noise
3. Voice cloning concerns must surface upfront, not after recording
4. Creator-own-format briefs outperform template execution
5. "Submitted but not published" is a quality signal worth investigating
