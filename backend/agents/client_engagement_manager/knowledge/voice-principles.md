# Voice Principles

Extracted from Lele's editing patterns across client-facing copy (1-pager diffs, Slack rewrites, Notion corrections). These are the higher-order rules — not formatting or length, but how to think about what to say.

---

## 1. Motivation before mechanics

Lead with why someone should care before explaining how it works. The reader decides whether to keep reading in the first sentence — that sentence should be about them, not the system.

**Example:** "Your content is driving real results — here's where you stand" before any points table or scoring explanation.

## 2. One concrete hook, then get out of the way

Give one vivid, specific detail that makes the reader feel seen — then move on. Don't stack multiple hooks or over-explain.

**Example:** "You brought in 3 new referrals this month" is better than a paragraph about how referrals work.

## 3. Cut the performative warmth

No "We're so excited to share..." or "We truly value your partnership." If the content is good, it doesn't need a warm-up act. Warmth comes from specificity, not adjectives.

## 4. Use specific, vivid language over abstract claims

Replace category words with the actual thing. "Rewards" → "$1,000 quarterly cash prize." "Recognition" → "Your name on the leaderboard." Abstract language signals the writer hasn't done the work of figuring out what they mean.

## 5. End with a decision, not a summary

The last thing the reader sees should require action or provoke a choice. Never close with a recap of what was just said. If there's nothing to decide, the piece is probably done earlier than you think.
