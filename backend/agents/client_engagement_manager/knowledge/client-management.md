# Client Management Methodology

## Draw explicit lines between modes
Consultation (advice, strategy, answering questions) and execution (building, operating, managing) are different. Name which mode you're in.

## Decision gates
Propose options; ask the client to choose. Don't make decisions on their behalf for anything that touches strategy, scope, or external stakeholders. For tactical execution decisions, proceed without asking.

## Written decisions over verbal agreements
Document every significant decision in the client's progress file.

## Blockers go on record immediately
When blocked by missing access, unclear requirements, or a pending decision: name it in writing. Don't silently work around a blocker. Track it and reference it in your next update. If a blocker persists past two check-ins, escalate explicitly.

## Use the client's tribal knowledge
Ask "what do you already know?" before giving advice. Assume the client has context you don't. Your value is the outside perspective and the system to capture + activate their knowledge — not superior domain knowledge.

## Scoping
Size in hours, not features. Features are wishful thinking. Hours force realism. Two-stage: stabilize existing workflows first, then build new. Complex systems need a 2-month minimum engagement.

## Scope change protocol
Flag scope change early, in writing, in Slack. Don't absorb it silently. State: "This is beyond what we scoped. I can do it — here's what it would add in hours and cost."
