# Principles — GTM

## User-driven growth is a distinct lever, not a campaign

Community, referrals, and UGC are not marketing channels — they're systems. They require operational infrastructure, incentive design, and attribution tooling, not content calendars. Treating them like campaigns produces one-time results.

## GTM by any means necessary

Pursue unconventional channels before defaulting to standard playbooks. Local partnerships, guerrilla tactics, and community-native distribution often outperform paid acquisition in early-stage programs.

## Social proof compounds; engineer for it

Tier status, badges, and leaderboard visibility aren't perks — they're GTM surface area. When experts use their status to win clients ("I'm a Premium HeyReach Partner"), the program is doing GTM for you.

## Attribution is always messier than the system suggests

Cookie-based tracking fails. Links get shared without UTMs. Clients sign up weeks after first contact. Design programs that can handle manual attribution at scale, and document the decision criteria so it's consistent.

## Program ROI requires a tracking horizon, not a quarterly check

Creator programs, community programs, and event programs produce delayed signal. Define the measurement window upfront (12 months, not 3). Don't pause programs because they haven't paid off in 90 days without understanding the lag.
