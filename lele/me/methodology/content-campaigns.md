# Methodology — Content Campaigns

## The Yarn experiment (reference case)

Ran 3 campaigns for Yarn's auto-audio-generation product with Clay's creator program (~200 active creators).

**Mechanic:** built demo assets using prebuilt audio profiles → sent creators a landing page to record a few seconds of their voice → generated a personalised demo in their voice → tracked who completed and posted.

**Tracking funnel used:** sent → opened → confirmed → submitted → published

**Results:** 10–20 posts out of 200 active creators per campaign. Not bad for a cold mechanic, not good enough to continue.

**Why it stopped:**
- Most creators disliked posting the same content as everyone else — homogeneity killed motivation
- Some had concerns about the likeness of their cloned voice
- 3 campaigns, same declining pattern each time

## What this tells you about creator campaigns

**Personalisation at the content level doesn't substitute for personalisation at the message level.** Creators who post UGC want to feel like they're expressing something true about themselves, not executing a template — even if the template is technically in their voice.

**Homogeneous campaigns have a ceiling.** The first few creators to post get engagement. Everyone after them is noise. If 200 people post the same thing in the same week, the audience sees through it immediately.

**Voice cloning concerns are real and should be surfaced upfront.** If the mechanic involves likeness or AI-generated content, creators need explicit framing before they agree — not after they've already recorded.

## What works better

- Give creators a brief with a clear angle, then let them execute in their own format
- Stagger posting — not everyone at once
- Segment by creator type before briefing — what works for a technical creator is different from a community-builder
- Track the funnel above but treat "submitted ≠ published" as a quality signal, not a failure — if a creator submits but doesn't post, ask why before the next campaign

## Channel by purpose

- **Email** — formal invites, onboarding, contractual
- **Slack/DM** — nudges, check-ins, unblocking
- **Channel announcements** — bulk updates, not asks that need individual responses

## Brief format

1 page max. Creators don't read walls of text. One brief = one ask. If the mechanic is complex, break it into steps across separate touchpoints.
